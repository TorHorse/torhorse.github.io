Drafting

Created Sunday 08 March 2020

[Flavor Description of drafting]

Each player chooses 3 Heroes from the pool.

After each player has chosen their heroes, the players are encouraged to discuss deckbuilding and drafting strategies based on the heroes they have chosen. (Example: "My hero is good with weapons" or "I'm going to try and build a spell and trap deck" or "I'm going to go for powerful late-game cards, can you guys help cover me in the early game?") Think of it like meeting up in the tavern to plan your adventure. After this discussion, move on to the actual draft portion.

  • Shuffle the Player Cards.
  • Each player makes 3 packs of 10 random cards.
  • Each player then adds 1 copy of each Generic Card to each pack, for a total of 15 cards.
  • Each player opens their first pack and chooses a card from it, then passes the remaining cards to the player on their left.
  • Continue taking cards in this manner until each player has taken 5 cards.
  • Proceed to the second pack and repeat these steps, passing cards to the right this time; and then to the left again on the third and final pack.
  • Once during each pack, each player may "Reserve" one extra card from a pack in their hand. This reserved card does not go into their card pool, but rather into a communal pool that can be shared among all players. Players may divvy up these 12 reserved cards however they choose after the draft

Actions

Created Saturday 09 May 2020

Actions are how your chosen Heroes will defend the [town?] from the evil hordes seeking to destroy it. Playing cards, attacking, defending, and activating abilities are all considered Actions in [game]. To take an Action, first choose the Hero who will be taking it, then pay any associated costs, and lastly make any decisions the Action asks you to make, like choosing a target or picking an effect. For example:

The [town?] is being attacked by a Skeleton Warrior in Thaddeus’ Lane. He chooses his hero Jek, Blademaster to take the Defend Action against the attack. The cost of the Defend Action is to [tap?] the hero taking it. After [tapping?] Jek, he chooses the target he is defending against, in this case the Skeleton Warrior. Now the Skeleton Warrior will damage Jek with it’s attack instead of the defenseless [townsfolk?]. Way to take one for the team Jek.

Most Actions can only be performed at certain times. Combat Actions, like Attacking and Defending, can only be taken during the Combat Phase. Fortification Actions, like playing or utilizing Fortification cards can only be taken during a Fortification Phase. Lastly there are Quick Actions, which are denoted by the [%] symbol, and can be taken during any phase. If an action does not have a type listed it is assumed to be a Fortification Action.

During combat, Thaddeus wants to play the Firebolt spell from his hand to finish off the Skeleton Warrior. Because Firebolt has the Quick Action symbol [%], he doesn’t need to wait until the next Fortification Phase to play it. First he chooses which of his Heroes will take the Action of playing Firebolt, in this case Clayre Philris. He then pays Firebolt’s resource cost from his Heroes’ Resource Pool, and finally chooses a target for Clayre to cast Firebolt on. Take that bonehead!

For a full list of what qualifies as an Action, see below:

  • Playing a card
  • Attacking
  • Defending
  • Activating the ability of a hero, item, equipment, or ally.

Influence

Created Saturday 09 May 2020

In addition to a resource cost, most cards also have an Influence requirement, indicated by the [SMA] next to their printed cost. This requirement represents the amount of influence you must have of the specified type among permanents or effects you control in order to play that card. This influence is not consumed or spent as resources are, you merely need to control it at the time of playing the card or activating the effect that requires it.
As a general rule most Heroes provide one influence of their associated color, though some may provide more, less, or different influence under certain circumstances. (see Ebbit and Barbotha)
